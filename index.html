bio I am a researcher at the Institute of Information Studies at the Jagiellonian University in Kraków. My work examines the intersections of information ecology, digital resistance, and the evolution of counterculture media. My research focuses on the management of information within pirate radio and underground journalism, specifically how these communities navigate the digital age. Methodologically, I integrate qualitative communication studies with computational and AI-driven research to analyse digital trace data and post-truth media environments.
research interests My research explores the tension between structured information systems and decentralised digital resistance, with a particular focus on the evolution of pirate radio, underground journalism, and clandestine communication in the digital age. I am deeply interested in information ecology and the sustainability of information environments, building on my doctoral work regarding data management in urban spaces. Methodologically, I seek to bridge qualitative media studies with computational and AI-driven research to analyze digital trace data, AI-driven misinformation, and post-truth media environments. Additionally, I investigate the strategic intersection of knowledge management and automation, specifically how low-code/no-code platforms and DevOps frameworks can be utilised to transform organisational and academic information systems.
